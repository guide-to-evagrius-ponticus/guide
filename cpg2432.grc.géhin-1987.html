Kephalaia gnostika
Capita gnostica
cpg2432
Third part of the trilogy (see Praktikos), comprising 540 chapters (90 chapters in six "centuries") discussing the highest levels of contemplation. This text, which is marked by philosophical erudition and written cryptically, is frequently considered to be behind the condemnations of Origen in 553. Because of how little survives in Greek, the Syriac translations are key, especially the so-called unexpurgated version (frequently called S2).
